Week 2

During the second week, the project transitioned from individual component testing to integrated system development. The aim was to stabilize the power output for electronic consistency and to successfully interface the accelerometer and GPS modules simultaneously to record a comprehensive dataset.

Software

This week’s software efforts were dedicated to refining our data parsing logic to handle real-world sensor outputs and launching our project's public-facing digital presence.

We implemented a critical firmware update after discovering our logging script was ignoring data from the modern dual-constellation GPS module, which utilized "$GNRMC" headers instead of the "$GPRMC" headers our code was originally expecting. We modified the code to catch both string types to ensure zero data loss and confirmed that the sensors were outputting reasonable values once physical connections were secured. Additionally, we successfully launched the first draft of our project’s web dashboard using HTML and CSS, providing a professional interface for future data visualization.

Hardware

To make the generated power usable for electronics, the coil was connected to a rectifier bridge to convert AC voltage to DC voltage. Because the resulting DC voltage was unstable and insufficient, a supercapacitor and an MT3608 boost converter were added to the circuit. We also successfully optimized the MT3608 boost converter discovering it required approximately 15-20 full anticlockwise turns to begin stepping up the voltage. It was noted that the high capacitance of the supercapacitor meant it took roughly ten minutes to charge the system to the required 3.3V.

The MPU6050 accelerometer was integrated via I2C at 400 kHz. After troubleshooting the Pico's breadboard seating issues to ensure stable power delivery, we deployed a backup GPS module to maintain momentum after the original unit overheated. By the end of the week, our dual-sensor integration was confirmed with a combined script successfully logging speed, distance, and 3-axis motion in real-time.
